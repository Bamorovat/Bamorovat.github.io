About Me

I am a researcher and engineer specializing in computer vision, sensor fusion, and robotics. I build robots that can see, understand, and move, and sometimes they find the door faster than I do before coffee. Currently, I am a Research Fellow at the University of Surrey, leading a NERC-funded project that uses autonomous, kite-tethered ground vehicles to map greenhouse gas emissions. It proves that robots can indeed fly kites for a cause.

My work focuses on turning multimodal perception into dependable autonomy. I completed a fully funded PhD at the University of Hertfordshire, where I advanced Human Activity Recognition and created the Robot House Multiview dataset. In industry, I have consistently worked to bring research out of the lab and into the field. As an Autonomy Scientist at AIDRIVERS and a Robotic Software Specialist at the University of Manchester (RAICo), I delivered real-time navigation and perception systems across wheeled, aerial, and underwater platforms. Previously, I built perception pipelines at Conigital and founded robotics R&D startups in Iran.

Teaching and mentoring remain a core part of my practice. With over a decade of teaching experience, including as a Visiting Lecturer at the University of Hertfordshire, I actively continue to mentor students. My goal is to help them move complex ideas from early concepts to deployed systems, bridging the gap between prototype research and the real world.

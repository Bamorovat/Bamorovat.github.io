About Me

I am a Robotic Software Specialist at the University of Manchester with over 15 years of experience in robotics, autonomous systems, and machine learning. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Hertfordshire, where I specialized in human activity recognition for ambient assisted living scenarios under the supervision of Prof. Farshid Amirabdollahian.


Currently, I work at the University of Manchester as a Robotic Software Specialist, focusing on cutting-edge robotics research and development. Previously, I worked as a Perception Team Engineer at Conigital, where I developed advanced driver monitoring systems, multi-sensor fusion algorithms, and deep learning models for autonomous vehicles. My expertise spans the entire perception pipeline, from sensor calibration to real-time processing and validation of safety-critical systems.


Throughout my career, I have successfully led research teams, published numerous peer-reviewed papers, and delivered innovative solutions in both academic and industrial settings. My passion lies in bridging the gap between theoretical research and practical applications, particularly in robotics and computer vision technologies that enhance human life and safety.
